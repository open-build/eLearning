Open Source

At its core work, OpenBuild helps to build and support new and existing Open Source software for nonprofit organizations and social businesses working in the humanitarian and development sector.

This includes supporting the development and implementation of a Open Source data management platforms

Our teams of two or more provide dedicated support to clients that either directly contract us to support their data management process and host their Open Source projects/systems or subcontract us through one of our partner Open Source projects.

In addition to our Open Source projects, OpenBuild has two initiatives that promote the use of open data standards and technologies.

Humanitarian Data Certification

We are working with industry leaders to help define and create new security policies and privacy standards for humanitarian data sets.

In this process, we will set up a new certification board that act as a certification authority for OpenBuild. In this capacity, the board will develop a process for certifying a particular type of organization, service provider or tool as having adopted the OpenBuild security and privacy standards.

This recognized certification will allow us to create standard auditing procedures and practices inside networked organization, cloud services or tools for securing information, cleaning and anonymizing beneficiary data and helping to prevent intrusions, and raising awareness of security needs.

In addition, we will help to define and continually evolve information security practices and give organizations both large and small a start in protecting themselves and their valuable data.

STEM Teaching

With OpenBuild being an active member in the Open Source community, we are deeply passionate about contributing to the spread of learning and knowledge of Open Source technologies, both software and hardware in our local communities.

Our developers, technical specialists, and analysts volunteer their time to work with local organizations and schools and organizations to teach STEM based courses.

In addition, we partner with local community members to help set up project spaces, offer master classes and open courseware as well as introduce projects that attempt to solve real-life challenges through STEM teaching.

All of these focus on Open Source software and hardware initiatives as well teaching and mentoring students and junior team members about process, best practices and more in the evolving tech industry.

For more information on ways to get involved with our STEM Teaching program, please contact us at: info@open.build
